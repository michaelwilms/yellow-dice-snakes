At Vrana Lake in northern Dalmatia, a strikingly yellow morph of the dice snake (Natrix tessellata) can be found. This form, known as flavescens, was first described by Franz Werner in 1891 and is characterized by a pale yellow base colour, intense black speckling, and red eyes and tongue. The morph appears to be genetically stable and is found regularly in the lake‚Äôs population. It is considered partially albinotic and may represent a localized variant of the species. The Vrana Lake region offers a rich mosaic of habitats and is home to a diverse herpetofauna.
